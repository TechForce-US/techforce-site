## Executive Summary

Tech Force LLC is a Kansas-based technology services and products company founded in 2023 by Jacob Stewart, a senior software engineer with 10 years of hands-on development and technical leadership experience. The company operates at the intersection of custom software development, strategic technical advisory, and AI-enabled product innovation.

Tech Force is positioned to serve two distinct markets: (1) small-to-mid-sized businesses that need senior engineering talent without the overhead of a full-time hire, and (2) niche vertical software buyers — specifically educational institutions using Moodle and cost-conscious homeowners seeking self-managed security systems.

The near-term priority is converting existing service revenue into recurring product revenue through the SIS platform, while growing the Fractional CTO and AI consulting practices to fund operations and build pipeline.

## Products

### Product 1: Student Information System (SIS)

**Overview:** A Moodle-integrated student information system featuring transcript generation, reporting, and academic records management. Initially built for Living Faith Bible Institute (LFBI).

**Market Opportunity:** Thousands of private schools, Bible colleges, and independent educational institutions run Moodle as their LMS but lack a purpose-built SIS layer. Commercial SIS platforms (PowerSchool, Alma, Infinite Campus) are priced for large public school districts and are overkill — and too expensive — for small private institutions.

**Go-to-Market:**
- Identify all accredited and non-accredited private schools using Moodle (initial target: ICP and similar institutions)
- Offer a freemium or low-cost hosted tier to drive adoption
- Monetize through monthly SaaS subscriptions and optional implementation/support packages

**Pricing Model (Recommended):**
| Tier | Price | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | $99/mo | Up to 200 students, core SIS features |
| Growth | $249/mo | Up to 500 students + transcript generation |
| Enterprise | Custom | Unlimited students, custom integrations, support SLA |

**Competitive Advantage:** Deep Moodle integration, purpose-built for small private institutions, low implementation burden, and pricing calibrated to smaller budgets.

**Risks:** Small TAM per institution requires volume; sales cycle may be slow in education. Mitigate by targeting a clear niche and keeping the product lean.

**Next Steps:**
- Harden the LFBI deployment as a reference implementation
- Build a self-serve demo environment
- Identify 5 prospective schools and begin outbound outreach
- Establish a pricing and billing infrastructure (Stripe)

---

### Product 2: Sound Security

**Overview:** A Zigbee-based smart home security hub/panel compatible with thousands of off-the-shelf Zigbee devices and RTSP cameras. Designed for full user self-setup — no professional installation required.

**Market Opportunity:** The DIY home security market is large and growing. Consumers are increasingly resistant to Ring/ADT-style monthly monitoring fees and locked hardware ecosystems. Sound Security targets privacy-conscious, technically capable homeowners who want local control, open standards, and no subscription lock-in.

**Differentiation:**
- Zigbee compatibility = massive device ecosystem at affordable prices
- RTSP camera support = works with existing cameras
- No forced cloud subscription or monthly monitoring fee
- Self-setup lowers cost and appeals to the DIY homeowner segment

**Go-to-Market:**
- Direct-to-consumer via online store (Shopify or similar)
- Communities: r/homeautomation, r/homeassistant, r/DIYsecurity, Home Assistant forums
- YouTube and content marketing around self-hosted security setups
- Consider Home Assistant plugin/integration for immediate credibility

**Revenue Model:**
- Hardware unit sale (primary)
- Optional cloud add-on (remote access, backup, notifications) — low-cost monthly subscription
- Accessory and expansion hardware over time

**Risks:** Hardware products carry inventory risk, supply chain complexity, and higher upfront capital requirements. Start with a limited pre-order run or crowdfunding campaign to validate demand before scaling production.

**Next Steps:**
- Define MVP hardware bill of materials and unit cost
- Identify contract manufacturing or initial production run strategy
- Build a landing page and capture early interest (email list)
- Evaluate crowdfunding (Kickstarter/Indiegogo) as a go-to-market mechanism

## Conclusion

Tech Force LLC has strong foundational assets: a credible founder, a live product in the market (SIS), an innovative hardware concept (Sound Security), and real client relationships. The primary risk is the typical early-stage founder challenge — too many directions, not enough bandwidth.

The recommended focus for 2026 is to:
1. **Scale the Fractional CTO practice** as the highest-margin, most scalable service.
2. **Convert the SIS into a multi-school SaaS product** with self-serve onboarding.
3. **Validate Sound Security demand** before committing capital to production.

Tight focus on these three will build sustainable recurring revenue and lay the foundation for hiring, product investment, and long-term growth.
